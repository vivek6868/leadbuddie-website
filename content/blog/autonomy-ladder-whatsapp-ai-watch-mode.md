Artificial Intelligence (AI) is transforming sales, but for a local business owner, letting an AI chat with your customers can be terrifying. 

*   *What if the AI promises a 50% discount?*
*   *What if it quotes a price for a product you do not stock?*
*   *What if it hallucinates and insults a long-term customer?*

These fears are completely valid. Most chat automation tools force you to go "all-in"—you switch the bot on, and hope it doesn't make a mistake. If it does, you only find out when the customer calls to complain.

We believe there is a better way. You shouldn't have to choose between full automation and manual chaos. Instead, you can use the **Autonomy Ladder** to safely transition your business to AI, maintaining complete control at every step.

---

## What is the Autonomy Ladder?

The Autonomy Ladder is a framework that allows you to configure your AI employee’s level of control based on your trust level. It features three distinct steps: **Watch Mode**, **Approval Mode**, and **Auto Mode**.

```
  [ STEP 3: AUTO MODE ]      ──► Full autonomy (Active 24/7 or Out-of-Hours only)
          ▲
  [ STEP 2: APPROVAL MODE ]  ──► AI drafts replies; human clicks "Send" to approve
          ▲
  [ STEP 1: WATCH MODE ]     ──► AI watches, learns, and drafts silently in background
```

---

## Step 1: Watch Mode (Zero Risk, High Learning)

When you first onboard an AI assistant, set it to **Watch Mode**. 

In Watch Mode, the AI is a silent observer. When a customer messages you, the AI drafts a response in the background, but **it does not send anything**. 

### How it works:
1.  A customer messages: *"Do you supply water filters in Noida?"*
2.  The AI drafts: *"Yes, we supply and service RO filters across Noida. Our standard installation is ₹499."*
3.  The draft stays hidden in your dashboard. You reply manually.
4.  At the end of the day, you look at the **Draft History** to compare your manual replies with the AI's drafts.

This mode carries **zero risk**. It allows you to audit the AI's knowledge and build confidence without ever exposing a draft to a live customer.

---

## Step 2: Approval Mode (The Collaborative Desk)

Once you see that the AI's drafts are 90% accurate, you promote it to **Approval Mode**.

In Approval Mode, when a customer messages, the AI drafts a reply and places it in your **Approval Desk**. The message is NOT sent to the customer until a human reviews it.

### How it works:
1.  A lead messages: *"Can you send a technician today?"*
2.  The AI drafts the booking details and creates a draft message.
3.  You get a notification on your dashboard. You click **Approve** to send the draft, **Edit** to tweak a detail (like the technician name), or **Delete** to ignore it.

Approval Mode gives you the speed of AI (since you do not have to type the message) with the absolute safety of human review. It is the perfect setup for busy retail floors.

---

## Step 3: Auto Mode (The Automated Employee)

When the AI consistently writes perfect drafts that you approve without editing, you move it to **Auto Mode**.

In Auto Mode, the AI behaves like a real employee. It answers queries, schedules bookings, and coordinates follow-ups in under 10 seconds, completely unsupervised.

### Best Practice for Auto Mode:
To maintain safety, configure Auto Mode to run **Out-of-Hours only** (e.g., from 8 PM to 9 AM and on Sundays). During the day, keep it in Approval Mode. This ensures that you get instant coverage when your shop is closed and you are asleep, while maintaining manual touch during standard business hours.

---

## Built-In Guardrails: Safety first

Even in Auto Mode, a professional AI CRM must have strict safety guardrails:
*   **Pricing Guardrails**: If the AI does not know the exact price of a service (e.g., standard AMC package: ₹2,499), it should never guess. It must politely defer: *"I will check with our billing team and get back to you shortly."*
*   **Human Takeover**: If a human team member types a manual message in the thread, the AI must instantly pause itself and step back.
*   **Manual Pause**: A one-tap button in the chat view to freeze the AI for a specific contact if a conversation becomes delicate or complex.
